In collaboration with the Smithsonian National Museum of Natural History (NMNH) we explored the use of augmented reality to activate museum objects currently on display. For this project, we specifically focused on the Gems and Minerals exhibitions.

NMNH excels in storytelling in the exhibition space and offers world-class research and extensive collections, our objective was to propose rapid proprototypes that can further enhance and enrich visitor experiences in the museum space.

Our project proposal revolved around an interactive installation that helps visitors learn about the gem collections at NMNH. The installation would be setup like a mirror that would detect visitors through facial/kinetic recognition. Once the installation has been activated, users would be exposed to a series of screens allowing them to personally select a mineral and to make their gem into jewelry. At the end of the experience, the user would have the opportunity to take a picture as a souvenir takeaway.

Overall, we wanted to create an experience that was fun and engaging to the visitor. We also wanted to use that opportunity to expose users to the origins and stories of the minerals to elevate learning outcomes

In collaboration with Yao Chang, Yui Tian, and Franky Wang.

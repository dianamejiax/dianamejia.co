Project Overview: In collaboration with the Smithsonian National Museum of Natural History (NMNH) we explored the use of augmented reality to activate museum objects currently on display. For this project, we specifically focused on the Gems and Minerals exhibitions.

NMNH excels in storytelling in the exhibition space and offers world-class research and extensive collections, our objective was to propose rapid prototypes that can further enhance and enrich visitor experiences in the museum space.

Our project proposal focused on a interactive installation that would encourage visitors to learn about the gem collections at NMNH. The installation would serve like a virtual 'mirror' that would detect visitors through facial/kinetic recognition. Once the installation has been activated, users would be exposed to a series of screens allowing them to personally select a mineral and make their gem into jewelry.

Roles: Concept Brainstorming, UI, Print Design

Collaborators: Yao Chang, Yui Tian, and Franky Wang.
